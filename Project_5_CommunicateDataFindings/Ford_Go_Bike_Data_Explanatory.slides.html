Usage statistics of Ford GoBike Rental Services¶

by Manthan Patel¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, we looked at the Ford GoBike service's rental data and tried to see what factors contribute to higher rentals. Our goal is to understand the impact of features in the dataset that contribute to the rental services.

Dataset Overview¶

We have taken the trip data of November 2019 and the data collected has the following features:
Trip Duration (in seconds)
Start Time and Date
End Time and Date
Start Station ID
Start Station Name
Start Station Latitude
Start Station Longitude
End Station ID
End Station Name
End Station Latitude
End Station Longitude
Bike ID
User Type (Subscriber or Customer – “Subscriber” = Member or “Customer” = Casual)
Bike Share for All (Bay Area residents who qualify for CalFresh, SFMTA Lifeline Pass, or PG&E CARE utility discount are eligible to join our Bike Share for All program for 5 dollars for the first year)

Distribution of the Duration of Bike Rental¶

From the data, we can see that most rentals lie below 3000 seconds (50 minutes) but there are a few cases where the bikes have been rented for very long durations. A logarithmetic representation of the duration shows this well. Below, we have plots on both axis - Normal scale & Logarthimetic scale.

Distribution of Bike Rental with Days and Hours¶

The bike rentals are more common during weekdays and in particular, during the rush hours of 7Am to 9AM and 4PM to 6PM. We can also observe a trend where most rentals in the weekends happen mostly in the afternoon around 12PM to 2PM.

Distribution of Customer Types and Geographic Locations¶

From our data, we can see that 77% of the users are subscribers and casual customers are just 23%. Moreover, we can see that bike rentals are from 3 areas of California - San Francisco, Oakland and San Jose. San Francisco has the highest number of users (around 76%) followed by Oakland (with 19%) and finally San Jose (with 5%). The ratio of subscribers over casual customers in San Francisco, Oakland and San Jose are around 3.2, 3.7 and 6.42. This shows San Jose has very less number of casual customers

Overall Summary¶

From the analysis of the Ford GoBike data, we can say that most of the rentals are around 500 seconds and very few above 3000 seconds. Moreover, the service can be seen availed from San Francisco, Oakland and San Jose in California. San Francisco has the highest number of users (around 76%) followed by Oakland (with 19%) and finally San Jose (with 5%). Most rentals happen around the peak hours of 7AM to 9AM and 4PM to 6PM and are more during the weekdays than on weekends. But this relationship is stronger for San Francisco and is weaker in Oakland and is weakest in San Jose. It can be seen that most users are Subscribers and a very few are casual customers. The subscribers use the services primarily during peak hours and on weekdays, thus supporting the statement made above. Casual customers on the other hand use the services more during weekends and also during odd hours. Surprisingly though, San Francisco has a higher percentage of casual customers using the rental service than subscribers in comparison to the Oakland and San Jose area. But due to the sheer number of users in San Francisco, the overall usage statistics is maintained. Another interesting deviation can be seen in San Jose, where the subscribers are seen using the service throughout the day and not only during peak hours.
